# Channel Style — ECRM

Channel ID: `ECRM`

## Posture

The Aurelune inbox is a quiet inbox. We don't send daily. We don't send "last chance!". We send when we have something worth saying, and the customer comes to expect that.

## Cadence

| Programme | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome series | 4 emails over 14 days | Introduce the AM/PM thinking, the testing philosophy, the founder, the loyalty programme |
| Routine builder | One-time, triggered after first PDP visit | Helps the customer assemble a starter routine |
| Replenishment | Triggered by purchase + product lifespan | "Your PM serum is approaching the end of its cycle." No urgency language. |
| Evidence update | Monthly | One new piece of testing data, new white-paper, formulator note |
| Editorial — "The Phase" | Monthly | Long-form newsletter — one science topic, one routine deep-dive, one customer story |
| Loyalty rewards | As earned | "You've unlocked…" — calm announcement, no fanfare |
| Limited launch | 2–4× per year | Hero launches only. Never small things. |
| Sale | 2× per year (anniversary + year-end) | Restrained. "Annual moment. 15% across the range, two weeks." |

## Subject-line voice

Short. Lowercase often. Declarative.

✅ _the pm phase, explained_
✅ _your evidence update, may 2026_
✅ _two cycles, eight weeks, your skin_
✅ _what we tested this quarter_
✅ _your routine, replenished_

❌ _🔥 LAST CHANCE — 50% off everything!! 🔥_
❌ _You won't believe what happened next…_
❌ _Hey [first_name], check this out!_

## Body voice

Same as everywhere else (see `tone-of-voice.md`) — but ECRM is where long-form science can stretch its legs. Customers who opt in to the evidence update have explicitly chosen depth.

## Personalisation

- Phase (AM / PM / both) — based on purchase
- Persona segment — Performance Pip / Ritual Rina / Science Sven / Gentle Gemma
- Replenishment cadence — based on product lifespan and last purchase
- Market — language, currency, claim tier

Never personalise on first name in the body copy (feels intrusive). Subject line is fine.

## Design

- Single-column, 600 px width
- Plenty of whitespace
- One hero image, never a carousel
- Body copy in `Inter` 16 px, line-height 1.5
- One primary CTA; secondary link only if it adds value

## Localisation

Templates are global; copy is local. Translation is by in-market copywriter, never machine. Each market lead approves before send.

ECRM is the channel with the smallest variance in approval time (3 working days average internal, 2 working days client) — because the format is standardised. New programmes (anything that isn't an existing template) follow the standard 5 + 3 working day flow.
