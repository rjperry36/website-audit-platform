# Channel Style — Out of Home

Channel ID: `OOH`

## Posture

Restraint. Aurelune's OOH is recognised at 30 metres by the white space, not by the size of the headline.

## The 5-word rule

If a hero headline takes more than five words, the headline is wrong. Strip it back until it fits.

✅ _Skincare in sync._
✅ _PM phase, formulated._
✅ _Two phases. One skin._
✅ _Renewal, on a schedule._
✅ _The evidence is the product._

❌ _Discover the science behind your skin's circadian rhythm with Aurelune's newest serum_

## Format playbook

| Format | Headline length | Visual |
|---|---|---|
| 48-sheet billboard | ≤5 words | Product on bone background, generous margin, mark bottom-right |
| 6-sheet street poster | ≤5 words | Product on ink background, mark top-left |
| Underground / metro carriage | 3-4 words + 1-line subhead | Editorial-style typography, no model |
| Bus side / wrap | ≤4 words across the whole side | High-contrast wordmark, no product |
| Digital OOH (motion) | 3-4 word loop | Hold each word for 1.5 sec, no scrolling marquee |

## What never appears on Aurelune OOH

- ❌ A model's face
- ❌ A QR code in the hero space (it can sit small in a corner if commercially needed)
- ❌ A promotional offer headline ("20% off!")
- ❌ A URL longer than `aurelune.com`
- ❌ Two products side-by-side
- ❌ "New" as the headline

## Localisation rules

OOH is the channel where localisation matters most because of language and context.

- **UK** — full English wordplay permitted; cultural references native to London/Manchester
- **US** — slightly more direct; East Coast vs West Coast tonal nuance handled by local creative
- **DE** — German typography demands tighter tracking; never compound-word a headline awkwardly
- **FR** — sentence elegance matters; the French version is almost always longer than English, so we redesign the layout — we don't squeeze
- **JP** — vertical layouts permitted (and often preferred); katakana for brand name, kanji for product line where appropriate. Approval by JP brand team mandatory.
- **CN** — minimal OOH presence (mostly airport / Tier-1 LCD). Simplified Chinese, brand name in Latin alphabet. Claims regulator-cleared per execution.

## Approval

OOH executions require **both** the Global Brand team AND the local-market team to approve. No exceptions.

- Internal review (Halo & Helix): ECD + global account lead
- Client review: Global brand director + local market director
- Average end-to-end approval window: 8–12 working days. Plan accordingly.
