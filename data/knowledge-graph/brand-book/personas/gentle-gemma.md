# Persona: Gentle Gemma

> *"I've got reactive skin. I just want something that doesn't irritate. I'd rather have one product I trust than ten that might work."*

**Archetype:** The sensitive-skin loyalist
**Age range:** 24–58 (broadest of the four)
**Income band:** £35k–£90k
**Primary motivation:** Won't react, won't sting, won't break me out

## Snapshot

Gemma has rosacea, eczema, peri-menopausal skin, fragrance sensitivity, or some combination. Her relationship with skincare has been characterised by elimination — she's tried products that promised gentleness and let her down. She is cautious, evidence-aware, and price-sensitive but willing to pay for reassurance.

She moves slowly. Patch tests for a week. Buys travel sizes before full sizes. Once she finds a product she trusts, she stays for years and tells friends.

## What she buys

| Behaviour | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Categories | Cleansers, moisturisers, ceramide creams, mineral SPF |
| Price ceiling | £70/SKU but starts with travel sizes (£15–£25) |
| Replenishment cycle | Long — 12 weeks+ |
| Where she buys | Pharmacy-adjacent retailers first; D2C once trusted |

## What works on her

- Dermatologist endorsements (named, not vague "expert-tested")
- Travel sizes and trial kits as low-risk entry points
- Allergen and fragrance information up-front on the PDP
- "Free with first order" sample policy
- Patch-test instructions baked into onboarding

## What turns her off

- "Powerful" / "active" / "potent" language
- Trendy actives without irritation data
- Surprise fragrance
- Influencer marketing
- "Bestseller" badges that don't tell her if it's for *her* skin type

## Channel fit

| Channel | Affinity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `SEO` | Very high | "ceramide cream rosacea", "fragrance-free moisturiser" |
| `D2C` | High | Once trust is earned; long LTV |
| `ECRM` | High | "Quiet" educational emails work; promotional spam does not |
| `POSM` | Very high | Reads the box, picks up the bottle, tests the texture |
| `SOCIAL_MEDIA` | Moderate | Follows dermatologists, not creators |
| `B2B` | Moderate | Salon recommendations from a trusted aesthetician are powerful |
| `EVENT` | Low | Unless it's a clinic-adjacent panel |
| `OOH` | Low | Won't drive consideration on its own |

## Market spread

Even spread across UK, US, DE, FR. Highest density in DE (pharmacy-led market). JP has a strong sensitive-skin culture but the local variant of Gemma reads more research and overlaps with Sven.
