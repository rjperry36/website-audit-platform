# Persona: Performance Pip

> *"If I'm going to spend £80 on a serum, I want to know exactly what's in it, why, and what changes I should be measuring by week eight."*

**Archetype:** The skin-invested optimiser
**Age range:** 32–45
**Income band:** £70k–£140k
**Primary motivation:** Visible, measurable results

## Snapshot

Pip works in a knowledge profession — consulting, product, finance, law — and applies that same evidence-led mindset to her skin. She has a routine she can recite from memory, a shelf she rotates seasonally, and a quiet competitiveness about being the colleague whose skin people compliment.

She doesn't want hype. She wants the panel size, the percentage, the weeks. She'll read the white-paper PDF if you publish one. She'll add it to the basket if she believes the chemistry.

## What she buys

| Behaviour | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Categories | Serums, retinoids, peptides, SPF — in that order |
| Price ceiling | £120/SKU; spends ~£600/quarter on skincare |
| Replenishment cycle | 6–10 weeks; rarely stockpiles |
| Where she buys | D2C if loyalty rewards are good; Sephora for the gift-with-purchase moments; never Amazon |

## What works on her

- Long-form PDP with citations
- Before/after with sample size and methodology disclosed
- Ingredient deep-dive editorial
- ECRM with new evidence or new formulation rationale
- White-paper download as a lead-gen offer

## What turns her off

- "Glow up overnight!" anything
- Influencer-led product launches with no clinical data
- Sets that bundle a hero with two products she doesn't need
- Loyalty programmes that gate the best perks behind aspirational tiers

## Channel fit

| Channel | Affinity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `D2C` | High | Will read everything on a PDP |
| `ECRM` | Very high | Opens evidence-heavy emails |
| `SOCIAL_MEDIA` | Moderate | Follows expert dermatologists, not lifestyle influencers |
| `PAID_MEDIA` | Low–Moderate | Banner-blind unless it shows specific evidence |
| `OOH` | Low | Will recognise the brand, won't be persuaded by it |
| `EVENT` | High | Will attend a science panel, not a launch party |
| `RESEARCH` | She IS the research panel | Frequently signs up |

## Market spread

Pip exists in every market. Highest density in UK, US, DE. In JP she skews slightly older (38–48) and reads the ingredient list with more scrutiny than in any other market.
