# Persona: Ritual Rina

> *"My morning and evening routines are how I hold the day. The products matter, but so does how they make me feel when I use them."*

**Archetype:** The mindful sensorialist
**Age range:** 28–40
**Income band:** £50k–£100k
**Primary motivation:** Daily ritual, sensorial pleasure, calm

## Snapshot

Rina has a meditation app on her phone and a candle in her bathroom. Skincare is part of how she punctuates her day. She values texture, scent, and the small choreography of a routine over chasing the newest ingredient. She'll happily stay with a brand for years if the products feel right and don't irritate.

She reads reviews to feel out the *experience* — not to dig into clinical data. She's loyal once won. She's brand-suspicious of anything that feels rushed.

## What she buys

| Behaviour | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Categories | Cleansers, mists, oils, balms, moisturisers, masks |
| Price ceiling | £90/SKU; spends ~£400/quarter |
| Replenishment cycle | 8–12 weeks |
| Where she buys | D2C for refills; department stores for first purchase (wants to smell/touch); subscribes to gift sets |

## What works on her

- Beautifully shot product on a calm surface
- "Routine of" content — morning ritual, evening ritual
- Subtle textural language: "balm", "veil", "essence"
- Limited-edition packaging or gift sets
- ECRM that arrives weekly with mood/seasonal cues
- Sets and gifts-with-purchase

## What turns her off

- Loud, clinical, "lab-coat" branding
- Percentages and panel sizes on the hero shot
- "Anti-ageing" framing
- Aggressive discounts (feels cheap)
- Influencers who feel performative
