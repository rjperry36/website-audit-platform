# Aurelune — Positioning

## Brand idea

**Skincare in sync.**

Aurelune is a prestige skincare brand built around a simple insight: skin does different things at different times of day. Our formulas are designed to work _with_ skin's circadian rhythm — protective and balancing in the morning, restorative and renewing at night.

## Positioning statement

For skin-invested adults who treat self-care as a serious daily practice, **Aurelune** is the prestige skincare brand that designs formulas around the skin's circadian rhythm — because, unlike single-formula skincare that ignores time of day, every Aurelune product is engineered for when it's used. Backed by chronobiology research and visible after two cycles.

## Brand pillars

| Pillar | What it means | How it shows up |
|---|---|---|
| **Science-led** | Every claim is anchored in a study, in-vitro or consumer panel. We over-explain rather than under-evidence. | Citations on PDPs. White-papers downloadable. "How we tested" panel on the box. |
| **Time-aware** | Products are designed AM-specific, PM-specific or "Anytime". Routines are sold as morning + evening sets. | AM/PM iconography on every pack. PDP filter: "Time of day". |
| **Quietly luxurious** | Premium without theatrics. The packaging is restrained; the formulas do the work. | Restrained palette, generous whitespace, no exclamation marks, no glitter. |
| **Globally precise** | One ingredient philosophy worldwide, but adapted formulations (and claims) per market because skin, regulation and humidity vary. | Different SPF formulations for JP vs UK. Adapted retinoid strengths for US. |

## Category context

We sit between mass dermo brands (CeraVe, La Roche-Posay) and luxury heritage (La Mer, Augustinus Bader). Closest competitive set: Augustinus Bader (price), The Ordinary (science narrative — but mass), Drunk Elephant (lifestyle), Tatcha (ritual).

## What we are not

- **Not a "clean beauty" brand.** We use safe synthetic ingredients where they outperform naturals. We will not claim "free-from" as a virtue.
- **Not aspirational lifestyle.** No yachts, no models in cashmere. The product is the hero.
- **Not gendered.** Skin is skin. We use second-person and avoid gendered language.
- **Not maximalist.** Eight hero SKUs, not eighty. Each one earns its place.
