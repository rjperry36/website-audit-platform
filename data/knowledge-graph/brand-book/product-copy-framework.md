# Aurelune — Product Copy Framework

Every Aurelune product page, pack copy, and editorial mention follows the same scaffold. It exists so any agency team member can write to the brand voice without re-inventing structure each time.

## The 5-line scaffold

```
[1] Phase tag         — AM phase / PM phase / Always-on phase
[2] Product name      — three words maximum
[3] One-line role     — what this product is for, in one sentence
[4] Evidence line     — one specific, citable claim
[5] Pairing line      — what it sits with in a routine
```

### Worked example: Retinoid PM Serum

```
PM phase.
Retinoid PM Serum.
A nightly renewal serum for visibly smoother skin texture.
0.3% encapsulated retinaldehyde — 84% of a 102-person panel reported smoother texture after 8 weeks.
Pairs with: Bakuchiol Day Fluid (AM) and Ceramide Repair Cream (PM, second step).
```

## Long-form PDP scaffold

Below the 5-line scaffold, expand into:

1. **What it does** (75 words max) — mechanism, plain language
2. **How we tested it** (100 words) — panel size, weeks, methodology
3. **Who it's for** (50 words) — skin type / phase / persona fit
4. **How to use** (5 steps, numbered, imperative-but-warm)
5. **Full ingredient list** — INCI names, with one-line plain-English notes on the top five actives

## Claims tiers

We grade every product claim into one of three tiers. The tier determines how it can appear in copy.

| Tier | Definition | Where it can appear |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Substantiated** | Backed by in-vitro study or ≥50-person consumer panel ≥4 weeks | Pack, PDP, OOH, paid media |
| **B — Indicative** | Backed by smaller study or ingredient-level published evidence | PDP, editorial, social — must be hedged ("supports", "helps") |
| **C — Anecdotal** | Self-reported single-user testimonial | Social UGC only, must be attributed |

Claims by market are reviewed against local regulation:

- **US**: FDA cosmetic claims only — no drug-language. "Reduces appearance of fine lines" ✓. "Reverses wrinkles" ✗.
- **EU (DE/FR)**: Cosmetic Regulation EC 1223/2009. Substantiation file required for every Tier A claim.
- **UK**: post-Brexit broadly aligned with EU; CMA guidance on environmental claims.
- **JP**: Quasi-Drug status applies to whitening / anti-ageing actives. Stricter scrutiny on "効果" (efficacy) wording.
- **CN**: cross-border via Hainan — most actives permitted; on-mainland registration limits some retinoids. We retail only via cross-border in CN.

## Words and phrases — at a glance

| Use | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Renewing, supportive, balancing | Anti-ageing, age-defying |
| Visibly smoother, firmer, brighter | Younger-looking, "10 years younger" |
| Comforted, soothed, nourished | Cured, fixed, repaired (skin doesn't "repair" in the medical sense) |
| Two cycles (8 weeks) | Overnight transformation |
| Phase | Routine step |

## Pricing tone

We are confident about price. We do not apologise for it; we do not crow about it. When asked, we explain:

> _Aurelune sits at the upper end of the prestige market. The cost reflects the actives, the testing, and the absence of compromise in formulation. We don't run promotions on the hero range._

Discounting is reserved for sets, gifts with purchase on the loyalty programme, and limited annual moments (anniversary, birthday gift).
