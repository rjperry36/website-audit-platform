# Aurelune — Tone of Voice

## Voice principles

1. **Precise, never vague.** We say "0.3% encapsulated retinaldehyde", not "powerful actives". Specifics build trust.
2. **Confident, never breathless.** We don't oversell. The product earns the claim.
3. **Warm, never cool.** Science can be human. We write to a smart adult, not at one.
4. **Plain, never plain-language-y.** We don't dumb things down; we explain them clearly. "Chronobiology" stays. "Circadian rhythm" stays. Then we tell you what they mean.
5. **Lower-case more often than not.** Sentence case in headlines unless a brand or ingredient name demands the cap.

## Lexicon

### Use

- "in sync", "rhythm", "ritual", "routine"
- "phase" (AM phase / PM phase / always-on phase)
- "formulated for", "designed for", "engineered for"
- "evidence", "study", "panel", "trial"
- "tested at" (we always say where)
- "earned" — products earn their hero status; we don't anoint them

### Avoid

- ❌ "magic", "miracle", "secret"
- ❌ "anti-ageing" → use "skin-renewing", "rejuvenating", "supportive" instead
- ❌ "natural" as a virtue word (we'll use it accurately when it applies, but not as a positioning lever)
- ❌ "clean" / "toxin-free" / "free-from" — these imply other brands are dirty/toxic, which is unfair and untrue
- ❌ Exclamation marks. Almost never. Once a campaign, maximum.
- ❌ "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "next-level" — vague and overused
- ❌ Aggressive imperatives ("Buy now!", "Don't miss out!") — we trust the customer

## Sentence shape

Short sentences. Mostly. Use the occasional longer sentence to develop an idea — to give the reader a moment of thought — and then return to short ones.

Lead with the noun, not the adjective. _"Encapsulated retinaldehyde — gentler than retinol, with the same renewal signal."_ Not _"Our gentle, encapsulated retinaldehyde, with the renewal signal of retinol."_

## Punctuation house style

- **En dashes** (—) for parenthetical asides. Not hyphens.
- **Oxford comma** in lists of three or more.
- **No double spaces**. Anywhere.
- **Numerals for measurements**: "8 weeks", "0.3%", "30 ml". Words for one through nine in prose.
- **% sign**, no space.

## Worked examples

**Headline / hero**
- 🟢 _Skincare in sync._
- 🟢 _The PM phase, formulated._
- 🟢 _Renewal, on a schedule._
- 🔴 _Unleash your skin's true potential!_ (overpromise, vague, exclamation)

**PDP first paragraph**
- 🟢 _Retinaldehyde works while you sleep. We've encapsulated 0.3% of it inside a slow-release peptide shell — so it absorbs gradually overnight, instead of all at once. After 8 weeks of use, 84% of a 102-person panel reported visibly smoother texture._
- 🔴 _Our amazing PM serum will transform your skin overnight, leaving you with a youthful glow!_

**Social caption**
- 🟢 _PM phase, day 56. Texture before and after. Two cycles, 0.3% encapsulated retinaldehyde, no other variables changed._
- 🔴 _OMG you guys need to try this!! Skin like a baby ✨_

**OOH headline**
- 🟢 _PM. (Capitalised, period, end.)_
- 🟢 _Two phases. One skin._
- 🔴 _Wake up to glowing skin!_

## Voice across moods

| Mood | Sound like |
|---|---|
| Default | An informed friend who happens to be a formulator |
| Educational (blog, white-paper) | A patient teacher who respects the reader |
| Customer service | Warm, concise, never apologetic-by-default |
| Crisis (recall, claim challenge) | Direct, fact-led, accountable. Never defensive. |
| Celebration (anniversary, launch) | Quiet pride, not balloons |
